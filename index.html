About the Organisation

A Legacy of Language & Literature

Assam Nepali Sahitya Sabha is the premier literary and cultural organisation of the Nepali-speaking community in Assam. Founded in 1945, we have been the torchbearer of Nepali language, literature, and cultural heritage across the region for generations.

We strive to connect the Nepali diaspora in Assam through literature, arts, and community programs, ensuring that our language and culture flourish across generations.

Our Mission

Preserve and promote Nepali language and literature in Assam through sustained cultural engagement.

Our Vision

A thriving Nepali literary community that bridges tradition with modernity for future generations.

Core Values

Cultural integrity, community service, literary excellence, and inclusive participation.

Reach

Active chapters across Assam with a network spanning the Northeast Indian Nepali community.

Our Journey

A History of Legacy

From humble beginnings to a celebrated institution — the story of Assam Nepali Sahitya Sabha.

1945
Foundation of the Sabha
A group of visionary Nepali literary figures in Guwahati established the Sabha to preserve Nepali language and culture in Assam.
1962
First Sahitya Patrika Published
Launch of the literary journal which became the voice of Nepali literature in Northeast India.
1980
Inaugural Sahitya Samaroh
The first annual literary festival, now the most celebrated Nepali cultural event in Assam.
2005
Diamond Jubilee Celebration
60th anniversary marked with state-wide cultural programs, awards, and a commemorative publication.
2024
Digital Expansion
Launch of digital archives, online publications, and virtual events to reach the global Nepali diaspora.
